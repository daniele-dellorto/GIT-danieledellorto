BLACK AND WHITE

“To be away from home
and yet to feel oneself everywhere at home;
to see the world,
to be at the centre of the world,
and yet to remain hidden from the world—impartial natures
which the tongue can but clumsily define.
The spectator is a prince
who everywhere rejoices in his incognito.”


Charles Baudelaire

COLOR

“Mere color,
unspoiled by meaning,
and unallied with definite form,
can speak to the soul
in a thousand different ways.”


Oscar Wilde
